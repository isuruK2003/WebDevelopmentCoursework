Importance of Coral Reefs

Among the planet's most valuable and diverse ecosystems are coral reefs. Millions of organisms may be living in and near reefs, according to scientific estimates. Coral reef animals and plants are currently being used to create a wide range of medications that may be used to treat bacterial infections, viruses, cancer, arthritis, and other illnesses in humans.

Threats to Coral Reefs

Numerous threats for coral reefs. Most of them involves human activities. One example is leaking fuels, anti-fouling paints and coatings, and other chemicals to the water sources. These chemicals can destrupt the growth of corals, and their reproductive system, resulting massive damages to coral reefs.

Conservation Efforts

Growing Corals

Organizations such as NOAA in US facilitates and leads the researches that are trying to develop technologies to develop new corals.

Removing Invasive Species

Invasive species like Algea can damage coral reefs. These algeas covers the corals and blocks light getting to them. By removeing such invasive species coral reefs can be protected.

How You Can Help

There are sevaral things that you can do to keep the coral reefs healthy.

Here are few of them;

  • Be responsible when diving to see corals
  • Dispose and recycle trash in the correct way.
  • Reduce the use of chemical fertilizers.
  • Participate or volunteer to beach cleanup activities.

There are many more, but the most important thing is to spread the word!
