Green Sea Turtle

The green sea turtle is a large, herbivorous marine reptile found in tropical and subtropical seas. Recognizable by its smooth, greenish-colored shell, it primarily feeds on seagrasses and algae. These turtles are crucial for marine ecosystems but are threatened by habitat loss, climate change, and poaching.

Cymodocea

Cymodocea is a genus of seagrasses found in shallow coastal waters. These marine plants form underwater meadows that provide essential habitats for marine life, including fish and invertebrates. Cymodocea species play a critical role in maintaining healthy coastal ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and contributing to carbon sequestration.

Sharks

Sharks are powerful, streamlined predators found in diverse marine environments. Known for their sharp teeth and keen senses, they play a vital role in maintaining oceanic balance by controlling prey populations. Sharks exhibit a wide range of sizes and behaviors, making them crucial to the health of marine ecosystems.

7/10

Sea Anemones

Sea anemones are colorful, soft-bodied marine invertebrates with tentacles that capture prey and defend against predators. They attach to surfaces like rocks or coral reefs and use their stinging cells to immobilize fish and small invertebrates. These creatures form symbiotic relationships with clownfish and contribute to the health of coral reef ecosystems.

8/10

Turtle grass

Turtle grass is a type of seagrass found in shallow, coastal waters of the Caribbean and Gulf of Mexico. It forms dense underwater meadows that provide essential habitats for marine life, including fish, crustaceans, and sea turtles. Turtle grass plays a crucial role in stabilizing the seabed and maintaining healthy marine ecosystems.

9/10

Harlequin Tuskfish

The Harlequin Tuskfish is a vibrant, medium-sized marine fish known for its striking blue teeth and vivid orange, blue, and white stripes. Found primarily in the coral reefs of the Indo-Pacific region, this carnivorous fish preys on invertebrates and small fish, playing an important role in maintaining reef health and biodiversity.

10/10

Ocellaris clownfish

The Ocellaris Clownfish, known for its striking orange body with white bands, is a small marine fish found in the warm waters of the Indo-Pacific. Famous for its symbiotic relationship with sea anemones, this clownfish gains protection from predators while providing its host with food and cleaning services, contributing to a balanced reef ecosystem.
